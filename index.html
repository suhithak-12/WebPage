Mission Statement

"Family Alliance for Mental Health Recovery provides mutual
support for people seeking mental health recovery for themselves
and their loved ones and promotes humane, wellness-oriented care."

What We Do

  • Connect with other families for mutual support and education
  • Share our experience of what worked and what did not for us and our loved ones
  • Foster a dialogue between family members and their loved ones diagnosed with mental illness
  • Fight stigma and discrimination
  • Share information about evidence-based treatment methods
  • Share tips for choosing the mental health provider and navigating the mental health system based on our personal experience
  • Share tips about reading and understanding medical literature based on our personal experience
  • Provide a space for uncensored conversation for everyone interested in the subject of mental health
  • Welcome members of all faith and religious backgrounds, as well as atheists
  • Collect personal stories (the position of the authors does not necessarily have to reflect the position of the FAMHR)

Our Core Message

A psychiatric diagnosis does NOT mean changing one’s life plans, abandoning
one’s dreams, inability to have family, and living as a chronically ill or disabled
person. Recovery happens more often than most people realize. Family support
plays a key role

What We Don't Do

  • Take money from pharmaceutical companies and producers of medical devices
  • Participate in terminological controversies (debates over the best words to use)
  • Give medical advice
  • Pill Shaming
  • Promote treatments that are not supported by scientific evidence (such treatments may be mentioned in personal stories)
  • Promote spiritual interpretations of experiences commonly described as “psychosis” (such interpretations may be found in personal stories)
  • Give advice on how to make somebody adhere to a treatment they do not want or how to commit somebody to a hospital or Assisted Outpatient Treatment against their will
  • Have affiliation with any religious or political group

About Us

The Family Allaince for Mental Health Recovery (FAMHR) was founded by a group of parents whose children were diagnosed with serious mental illness (SMI) or with psychosis. It includes people who have been diagnosed with SMI or psychosis, their family members and friends, and allies. See our stories here.
